Cycloidal Drive

Designed & fabricated a 22:1 cycloidal gearbox to reduce a NEMA-17 stepper motor

ACTIVE PROJECT -- Reach out with any questions, but more updates here soon!

1 / 3

Note: As stated above this is an active project. This project is a part of a larger project of a robot arm which I am working on.

I discovered cycloidial gearboxes a few months back and decided I needed to build one. I had been contimplating the robot arm project for a while at this point and I decided to combine these two projects, as the minimal backlash and the potential for a significant reduction in a small form factor was quite appealing for a robotic joint.

I designed this from scratch in SolidWorks, and I prototyped in PETG (orange) and PLA (Yellow) on my Prusa CORE ONE FDM 3D printer. Figure 2 above is a video of the gearbox hooked up to a NEMA 17 stepper motor powered by a 24V power supply, (both items I salvaged from an old 3D printer), and controlled with an Arduino UNO Rev3 and an A4988 stepper driver, all wired up in a simple breadboard circuit. If the stepper sounds odd, note that I made my desk from a door and so it is hollow and resonates weird.

Again, I am currently working on this project and will upload more soon as I go, but please feel free to reach out with any questions.
